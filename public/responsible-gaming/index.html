Responsible gaming

Gambling should be fun — a form of entertainment, never a way to make money or fix money problems. DBBet is for adults 18 and over. This page explains how to stay in control and where to get help in Kenya.

Play safe: the basics

  • Set a budget before you play and never bet money you can't afford to lose.
  • Never chase losses. Trying to win back what you've lost usually makes it worse.
  • Set a time limit and take regular breaks.
  • Don't gamble when you're upset, stressed, or under the influence of alcohol.
  • Keep it separate from money for rent, food, school fees or bills.
  • It's not income. Treat any winnings as a bonus, not a plan.

Warning signs

Gambling may be becoming a problem if you:

  • Spend more money or time than you meant to.
  • Borrow money or sell things to gamble.
  • Lie to family or friends about how much you gamble.
  • Feel anxious, guilty or low after playing.
  • Gamble to escape problems or to win back losses.

If any of these sound familiar, it may be time to take a break or ask for help.

Tools to stay in control

Most licensed betting platforms, including DBBet, offer controls along these lines — check your account settings for what's available to you:

  • Deposit limits — cap how much you can deposit per day, week or month.
  • Time-out — lock yourself out for a short cooling-off period (e.g. 24 hours to a few weeks).
  • Self-exclusion — close access to your account for a longer period (six months or more) when you need a real break.
  • Reality checks — reminders of how long you've been playing.

To set any of these up, contact our team via the contact page and we'll help you put them in place.

Protecting under-18s

It is illegal for anyone under 18 to gamble. If children have access to your device, use device-level parental controls and never leave your account logged in. Keep your password and M-Pesa PIN private.

Where to get help in Kenya

  • NACADA (National Authority for the Campaign Against Alcohol and Drug Abuse) — toll-free helpline 1192. NACADA offers counselling and referrals, including for gambling-related harm.
  • Befrienders Kenya — 0722 178 177. Emotional support if you're feeling overwhelmed or in distress.
  • Talk to someone you trust — a friend, family member or a professional counsellor. You don't have to deal with it alone.

If you or someone else is in immediate danger, contact emergency services right away.

Need a break now?

You can pause or close your account at any time. Reach out through our contact page and ask for a time-out or self-exclusion — no questions, no pressure.
