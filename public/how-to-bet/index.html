Step 3: Understand the odds

Odds tell you two things: how much you can win, and roughly how likely an outcome is. DBBet uses decimal odds, which are easy to read:

  • Odds 2.00 — a KES 100 bet returns KES 200 (KES 100 profit).
  • Odds 1.50 — a KES 100 bet returns KES 150 (KES 50 profit). More likely to win, smaller reward.
  • Odds 4.00 — a KES 100 bet returns KES 400 (KES 300 profit). Less likely, bigger reward.

Simple formula: potential return = stake × odds.

Step 4: Pick a market

A "market" is what you're betting on. The most common football markets are:

  • 1X2 (match result) — home win (1), draw (X) or away win (2).
  • Over/Under goals — will the total goals be over or under a number (e.g. Over 2.5).
  • Both teams to score (GG/NG) — will both teams score, yes or no.
  • Double chance — cover two of the three results for a safer, lower-odds bet.
  • Correct score — predict the exact final score (harder, higher odds).
  • Handicaps — give one team a virtual head-start or deficit.

Step 5: Single bet or multibet?

  • Single bet — one selection. It wins or loses on its own. Lower risk.
  • Multibet (accumulator) — several selections in one bet. The odds multiply for a bigger payout, but every selection must win. Higher risk, higher reward.

Step 6: Place the bet

  1. Choose a sport, match and market, then tap the odds to add the selection to your betslip.
  2. Enter your stake — the betslip shows your potential return automatically.
  3. Tap Place bet. Your bet appears in your bet history.

Step 7: Live betting & cash out

Live (in-play) betting lets you bet while a match is happening, with odds that change in real time. Cash out, where available, lets you settle a bet early — locking in a profit or cutting a loss before the match ends.

Smart betting tips

  • Set a budget and stick to it. Betting is entertainment, not income.
  • Start with markets you understand, like 1X2 or Over/Under.
  • Don't chase losses — take a break instead.
  • Compare odds and look for value, not just favourites.
  • Read the responsible gaming guide and use deposit limits.

Frequently asked questions

How do odds work in betting?
Odds show your potential return. At odds of 2.00, a KES 100 bet returns KES 200 — your KES 100 stake plus KES 100 profit. Higher odds mean a bigger potential win but a lower chance of winning.
What is a multibet?
A multibet (accumulator) combines several selections into one bet. The odds multiply for a bigger payout, but every selection must win for it to pay out.
